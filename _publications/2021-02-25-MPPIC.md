![CFD-DEM vs MPPIC particle field comparison](../images/mppic_preview.png)

This paper compares two Eulerian-Lagrangian methods, CFD-DEM and MPPIC, for simulating biomass gasification in a fluidized bed. Drying, pyrolysis, and homogeneous/heterogeneous chemical reactions are implemented on top of both methods, and particle trajectories, diameters, heat transfer and temperatures are tracked. Validated against experiments, the difference between MPPIC and CFD-DEM in predicting the H2 share is no more than 0.26%, and both methods show consistent trends when varying the steam-to-biomass ratio and reactor temperature. The results suggest that, for reactor-scale predictions such as product gas composition, the cheaper MPPIC provides accuracy comparable to the particle-resolved CFD-DEM.
